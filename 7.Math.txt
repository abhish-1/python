Types of Events;Mutually exclusive, exhaustive, independent, and dependent events; used to decide which probability rule is valid. 
Complementary Rule;P(Aᶜ) = 1 − P(A): probability that event A does not happen. 
Addition Rule;P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A ∩ B): probability that A or B or both occur. 
Multiplication Rule;P(A ∩ B) = P(A)P(B | A); if A,B independent, P(A ∩ B) = P(A)P(B). 

Conditional Probability;P(A | B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B): probability of A within the restricted world where B has already occurred. 

Law of Total Probability;P(A) = Σᵢ P(Bᵢ)P(A | Bᵢ) for a partition {Bᵢ}. Break A into mutually exclusive cases and add them. Mutually exclusive + exhaustive (one of them must occur)

Bayes Theorem; P(A | B) = (P(B | A)P(A)) / P(B): update probability of cause A after observing evidence B.
